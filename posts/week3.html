🗞️ Week 3 in One Breath

The final stretch of the group stage somehow managed to feel like both a football tournament and an Excel exam. Some teams cruised through, some scraped through, and others spent the last few minutes of the group stage staring at live tables like interns trying to understand a pivot table five minutes before a meeting.

Argentina finished the group stage perfectly, Messi came off the bench to score a free kick against Jordan, and they now face Cape Verde in the Round of 32. DR Congo produced one of the great tournament stories by coming from behind to beat Uzbekistan 3-1, earning their first ever World Cup win and setting up a knockout tie with England.

Iran were knocked out despite going unbeaten, after Austria’s 96th-minute equaliser against Algeria completely ruined the spreadsheet. South Korea, Scotland and Uruguay also packed their bags, proving once again that hope is a dangerous and largely admin-heavy activity.

🐆 Team of the Week: DR Congo

DR Congo came from behind to beat Uzbekistan 3-1. It was their first ever World Cup win, and it sent them into the knockouts.

Naturally, their reward for one of the greatest days in their football history is a Round of 32 tie against England. Football has a wicked sense of humour.

🐐 Messi Admin Update

Argentina rotated heavily against Jordan and still won 3-1. Messi started on the bench, came on, scored a free kick, and once again made everything look annoyingly simple.

At this point, he is less of a footballer and more of a recurring calendar notification. You can dismiss it, but it will simply return next week and ruin your plans.
