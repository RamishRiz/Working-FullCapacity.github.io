Week 4 Edition

Late winners, semi-final statements, and one team suddenly looking terrifyingly inevitable.

From Final Eight to Final Boss.

If this stage of the 2026 World Cup has taught us anything, it’s that surviving is one thing, but controlling a tournament is another. The quarter-finals gave us tension, late drama, and heavyweight exits. The semi-finals gave us a full-blown statement.

We started with France, Morocco, Spain, Belgium, Norway, England, Argentina, and Switzerland. We are now down to the final stretch, and Spain have already booked their place in the World Cup final after a commanding 2–0 win over France in Dallas.

🎵 Song of the Week

This week’s pick: Pressure — David Bowie & Queen

Because this week was basically ninety-minute stress tests with studs on. Late winners, collapsing nerves, and one semi-final where Spain made pressure look like an art form.

👑 Spain didn’t just win — they took over

There are wins, and then there are warnings to the rest of the world. Spain beat France 2–0 in the semi-final, and the scary part was how comfortable they made it look.

Mikel Oyarzabal opened the scoring from the penalty spot, Pedro Porro added the second, and France never really looked like a side in control of their own fate. Spain strangled the game, shut down the spaces, and reached the final looking less like underdogs and more like the team everyone should have feared earlier.

They have now reached their first World Cup final since 2010, and they got there by flattening one of the tournament favourites. Not stealing it. Not surviving it. Owning it.

⏰ Merino Time: Belgium’s golden goodbye

Spain 2–1 Belgium

Belgium were minutes away from dragging Spain into something much uglier. Then Mikel Merino arrived late, again, and changed the ending.

Spain beat Belgium 2–1 in the quarter-finals with an 88th-minute winner, a brutal ending for a Belgian side that was leaning on the final World Cup push of its old guard. It was the kind of defeat that felt like more than a result — it felt like the closing credits on a generation.

Kevin De Bruyne, Romelu Lukaku, Axel Witsel and Thibaut Courtois have carried Belgium through more than a decade of major tournaments, and this looked painfully like the last World Cup chapter for several of them.

🇫🇷 Morocco’s dream met France’s machine

France vs Morocco

Morocco came into the quarter-finals carrying belief, momentum, and the full emotional backing of everyone who loves a team that refuses to know its place.

France, however, remained France. Clinical where it mattered, ruthless when the opening came, and too deep in quality to wobble for long. Morocco’s run deserved every bit of applause it got, but this was the stage where the margins got meaner and the champions of chaos ran into one of the tournament’s superpowers.

The fairytale was glorious. France were just less interested in poetry than progress.

🧨 England vs Norway: the heavyweight with studs on

Quarter-final spotlight

By the time the quarter-finals rolled around, this had become the glamor tie: Harry Kane against Erling Haaland, England’s nerve against Norway’s new belief, and an entire nation preparing itself to age horribly over two hours.

Norway arrived as the team nobody wanted after dumping Brazil. England arrived as the team that never, ever make things simple. It had all the ingredients of a proper knockout brawl: elite forwards, emotional instability, and the very real chance that one mistake would become national folklore.

If football ever wanted to market pure tension, this was the trailer.

🐐 Messi still bends tournaments around himself

There are players in tournaments, and then there is Lionel Messi turning tournaments into mythology.

Argentina’s route through the knockouts has continued to orbit around him. Even when the script goes wrong, even when the game gets frantic, even when the moment looks like it might finally belong to someone else, Messi drags the spotlight back with him.

At this point, asking whether he can be stopped feels less like analysis and more like wishful thinking from everybody else.

🌟 Stars doing star things

  • Mikel Oyarzabal — Opened the scoring against France and helped send Spain into the final.
  • Pedro Porro — A full-back scoring in a World Cup semi-final is one thing. Doing it while your team controls the whole match is another.
  • Mikel Merino — Late goals and knockout drama are becoming a personal brand.
  • Kylian Mbappé — France went out, but every opponent still spent the night worrying about him.
  • Lionel Messi — Still the gravitational force of Argentina’s campaign.

📌 Storylines nobody can ignore

  • Spain are no longer a “nice football” side. — They are organised, ruthless, and now one win from the trophy.
  • Belgium’s old era is fading. — The quarter-final loss felt like the end of something bigger than one tournament.
  • France looked human at exactly the wrong time. — Spain gave them no rhythm, no freedom, and no rescue.
  • The final is now staring everyone in the face. — And Spain look like the team setting the terms.

🔭 Looking ahead

We are now beyond quarter-final hypotheticals and into proper history territory. Spain have made the final. Everyone else is either chasing them, dreading them, or both.

  • World Cup Final: Spain vs England or Argentina
  • Third-place play-off: France vs England or Argentina

One side has already delivered a semi-final masterclass. The question now is whether anyone left can survive it.
